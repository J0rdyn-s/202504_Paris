Bateaux Parisiens Cruise Route

🚣️ Cruise Route: Bateaux Parisiens

🔶 Orange route: Sightseeing Cruises & Dinner Cruise (6:15 pm, 9:00 pm)
⚫ Black route: Lunch Cruise (12:45 pm), Dinner Cruise (8:30 pm), Special Cruises
※ Your cruise will follow the orange route only.

  • 1. Eiffel Tower (La Tour Eiffel, 1889)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Built for the 1889 Paris Exposition, this iron tower named after Gustave Eiffel stands at 330 meters and is a symbol of Paris. Famous for its nighttime lights and panoramic views.

  • 2. Les Invalides (1676)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Commissioned by Louis XIV as a hospital and home for wounded soldiers. It now houses military museums and Napoleon's tomb beneath its golden dome.

  • 3. National Assembly (L’Assemblée Nationale, 1798)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Originally a palace for the Duke of Bourbon, this neoclassical building now hosts the French National Assembly, the lower house of parliament.

  • 4. Musée d'Orsay (1900)

    🗺️ View on Map

    A former railway station transformed into a museum, showcasing Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art by Van Gogh, Monet, and Renoir.

  • 5. Institut de France (1688)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Home of the Académie Française, this domed structure is a pillar of French language and culture, known for its classical architecture.

  • 6. Notre-Dame Cathedral (1163)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Masterpiece of Gothic architecture famous for its rose windows and twin towers. Severely damaged by fire in 2019, it's under restoration.

  • 7. Paris City Hall (1882)

    🗺️ View on Map

    The central seat of the Paris administration since medieval times, reconstructed in Renaissance style after a 19th-century fire.

  • 8. Conciergerie (14th Century)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Originally part of a royal palace, later used as a prison during the French Revolution, notably housing Marie Antoinette.

  • 9. Louvre Museum (1546 / 1793)

    🗺️ View on Map

    One of the world's largest and most visited museums, showcasing works from ancient civilizations to the 19th century. Its glass pyramid is an iconic symbol.

  • 10. Place de la Concorde (1772)

    🗺️ View on Map

    The largest public square in Paris, once the site of royal executions during the Revolution, now home to the Luxor Obelisk.

  • 11. Grand Palais (1900)

    🗺️ View on Map

    A vast glass and iron exhibition hall built for the 1900 World Fair. Hosts prestigious cultural exhibitions and events.

  • 12. Arc de Triomphe (1806–1836)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Commissioned by Napoleon to honor military victories, the arch bears the names of generals and hosts the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier.

  • 13. Palais de Chaillot (1937)

    🗺️ View on Map

    Built for the 1937 International Exhibition, this cultural complex offers prime views of the Eiffel Tower from Trocadéro Gardens.
